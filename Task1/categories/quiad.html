Mazar-e-Quaid

About Mazar-e-Quaid

Mazar-e-Quaid, also known as the Mausoleum of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, is the final resting place of the founder of Pakistan. Located in Karachi, Pakistan, it is a significant national monument and an iconic symbol of the country's independence.

The mausoleum is built with white marble and features a grand dome and minarets. Surrounding the mausoleum are beautiful landscaped gardens, adding to the serene and peaceful ambiance of the site. Mazar-e-Quaid is not only a place of remembrance but also serves as a place for visitors to learn about the life and achievements of Quaid-e-Azam.

Interior of Mazar-e-Quaid

Inside the mausoleum, visitors can pay their respects to Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. The main chamber houses the tomb of Quaid-e-Azam, adorned with intricate carvings and a beautiful chandelier hanging from the ceiling. The walls are adorned with Quranic verses and quotations from Quaid-e-Azam, reminding visitors of his vision for a united and prosperous Pakistan.
